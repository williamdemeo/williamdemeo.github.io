Research papers in UA/CSP

  • 2010
    • Berman, Idziak, Markovic, McKenzie, Valeriote, Willard, Varieties with Few Subalgebras of Powers, Trans AMS 362 (3). The dichotomy conjecture has been confirmed for a certain class of finite algebras A which, among other things, have the property that the number of subalgebras of A^n is bounded by an exponential polynomial. This paper characterizes the finite algebras A with this property, and shows that they are the finite members of a newly discovered Mal'tsev class defined by the existence of "edge" terms.
  • 2009
    • Barto and Kozik, Constraint Satisfaction Problems of Bounded Width, Proc. 50th IEEE Symp Foundations Comp Sci, provides a characterization of applicability of The Local Consistency Checking algorithm to solving the non-uniform CSPs. (Settles the conjecture of Larose and Zadori.)
    • Barto, Kozik, Niven, The CSP Dichotomy Holds for Digraphs with No Sources and No Sinks, SIAM J Comput 38 (5), proves the CSP for a digraph with no sources or sinks is tractable if each component of its core is a cycle and is NP-complete otherwise.
    • Kozik, A 2EXPTIME Complete Varietal Membership Problem, SIAM J Comput 38 (6). Abstract: We construct a finite algebra generating a variety with 2EXPTIME complete membership problem. This proves that the universal membership problem for varieties and the varietal equivalence problem are 2EXPTIME complete as well, answering the question of Bergman and Slutzki from 2000.
    • Larose and Tesson, Universal algebra and hardness results for constraint satisfaction problems,Theoret Comp Sci 410 presents algebraic conditions on constraint languages that ensure the hardness of the associated constraint satisfaction problem for complexity classes L, NL, P, NP and ModpL.
    • Larose, Valeriote, Zadori, Omitting Types, Bounded Width and the Ability to Count Intl J Algebra and Computation 19 (5), shows that for CSP's of finite signature, the bounded width conjectures stated in Feder-Vardi, Larose-Zadori (below), and Bulatov are identical.
  • 2008
    • Maroti and McKenzie, Existence theorems for weakly symmetric operations AU 59.
      Proves Valeriote's conjectures:
      (i) any locally finite variety omits congruence covers of type 1 iff it satisfies WNU(k) for some k > 1;
      (ii) any locally finite variety is congruence meet SD if and only if for some k, it satisfies WNU(m) for all m > k-1.
      The result proved -- that a locally finite variety V omits type 1 iff V |= WNU(k) for some k > 1 -- is the simplest known Mal'tsev characterization of this family of varieties.
      Also demonstrates that every CD variety satisfies WNU(k) for all k > 2 (requires no locally finite assumption). In particular, NU(n) implies WNU(k) whenever k > 2 and n > 2.
    • Markovic and McKenzie, Few subpowers, congruence distributivity and near-unanimity terms, AU 58. Abstract. We prove that for any variety V, the existence of an edge-term and Jonsson terms is equivalent to the existence of a near-unanimity term. We also characterize the idempotent Mal'tsev conditions which are defined by a system of linear absorption equations and which imply congruence distributivity.
  • 2007
    • Kiss and Valeriote, On Tractability and Congruence Distributivity, Logical Methods Comp Sci 3 (2:6), proves constraint languages consisting of relations invariant under a short sequence of Jonsson terms are tractable.
    • Kozik, Computationally and Algebraically Complex Finite Algebra Membership Problems , Intl J Algebra and Computation 17 (8) Abstract: In this paper we produce a finite algebra which generates a variety with a PSPACE-complete membership problem. We produce another finite algebra with a gamma function that grows exponentially. The results are obtained via a modification of a construction of the algebra A(T) that was introduced by McKenzie in 1996.
    • Larose and Zadori, Bounded width problems and algebras, AU 56, shows that if a CSP has bounded width then the variety generated by the associated algebra omits the Hobby-McKenzie types 1 and 2; this provides a method to prove certain CSP's do not have bounded width. Applications: various graph homomorphism problems do not have bounded width; there is a finite order-primal algebra whose variety admits type 2 but omits type 1 (assuming P is not NP).
  • 2006
    • Bulatov and Dalmau, A Simple Algorithm for Mal’tsev Constraints SIAM J Comput 36 (1). The first algorithm solving the arbitrary constraint satisfaction problem with Mal’tsev constraints is very sophisticated and relies heavily on advanced algebraic machinery. This paper gives a simpler algorithm for this type of constraint.
    • Dalmau, Generalized Majority-minority Operations are Tractable, Logical Methods Comp Sci 2 (4:1). Abstract: Generalized majority-minority (GMM) operations are introduced as a common generalization of near unanimity operations and Mal'tsev operations on finite sets. We show that every instance of the constraint satisfaction problem (CSP), where all constraint relations are invariant under a (fixed) GMM operation, is solvable in polynomial time. This constitutes one of the largest tractable cases of the CSP.
    • Larose and Zadori, Taylor Terms, Constraint Satisfaction and the Complexity of Polynomial Equations Over Finite Algebras Intl J Algebra and Computation 16 (3). Abstract: We study the algorithmic complexity of determining whether a system of polynomial equations over a finite algebra admits a solution. We characterize, within various families of algebras, which of them give rise to an NP-complete problem and which yield a problem solvable in polynomial time. In particular, we prove a dichotomy result which encompasses the cases of lattices, rings, modules, quasigroups and also generalizes a result of Goldmann and Russell for groups.
  • 2005
    • Krokhin, Bulatov, Jeavons, The complexity of constraint satisfaction: an algebraic approach, (Notes taken by Alexander Semigrodskikh) Proc NATO ASI: Structural Theory of Automata, Semigroups and Universal Algebra Montreal 2003. Survey paper.
    • Bulatov, Jeavons, Krokhin, Classifying the Complexity of Constraints Using Finite Algebras, SIAM J Comput 34 (3), introduces a notion of tractable algebra; investigates how tractability relates to the tractability of the smaller algebras derived from it; classifies all finite strictly simple surjective algebras with respect to tractability, obtaining a dichotomy theorem which generalizes Schaefer's for the generalized satisfiability problem; suggests a possible algebraic criterion for distinguishing the tractable and intractable cases of CSP.
  • 2003
    • Bulatov and Dalmau, Towards a Dichotomy for the Counting Constraint Satisfaction Problem Proc. 44th IEEE Symp Foundations Comp Sci. This paper is about the counting CSP (or #CSP): given a first-order formula consisting of a conjunction of predicates, determine the number of solutions to the formula. They prove that if a subclass of the #CSP is tractable, then it must have a Mal’tsev polymorphism; this explains all existing complexity results for particular cases of #CSP, and leads to a conjecture about distinguishing tractable #CSPs; a dichotomy theorem for the complexity of #CSP on a 3-element domain is given, as well as new proofs of dichotomy results for counting graph homomorphisms.
  • 1982
    • Demel, Fast algorithms for finding a subdirect decomposition and interesting congruences of finite algebras, Kybernetika 18 (2). A fast algorithm is suggested for finding a subdirect decomposition of a given finite algebra into subdirectly irreducible ones. As a by-product, fast algorithms are presented for finding some interesting congruences of the given algebra.
