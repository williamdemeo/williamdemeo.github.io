The Algebraic CSP Dichotomy Conjecture

Oct 14th, 2014

The Conjecture

The CSP associated with a finite idempotent algebra A is tractable iff A has a WNU term operation.

Let A be a finite idempotent algebra and let V(A) denote the variety generated by A. Then, the following are equivalent:

  • The TCT type set of V(A) omits type 1.
  • V(A) has a Taylor term operation.
  • V(A) has a WNU term operation.
  • V(A) has a cyclic term operation.
  • V(A) has a 4-place Siggers term operation.
  • No “divisor” of A is a two element algebra with only the trivial (projection) operations.

It is known that if a CSP is tractable, then the associated algebra A must satisfy the equivalent conditions above. (See the section No tractability without Taylor terms below.) The converse is open. That is, it is not known whether each of the equivalent conditions on A above is sufficient to prove tractability of the associated CSP.

Taylor terms

Walter Taylor proved in [1] that a variety V satisfies some nontrivial Malcev condition iff it satisfies the following one: for some $n$, V has an $n$-ary term operation $t$ such that, for all $i$ between 1 and $n$ there is an identity true in V where different variables $x\neq y$ appear in the $i$-th position on either side of the identity. (Such a term $t$ is called a “Taylor term”.)

Taylor iff no Type 1

Hobby and McKenzie proved in [2] proved that a finite algebra A has a Taylor term iff 1 does not belong to the TCT type set of V(A).

Type 1 iff Trivial 2

Freese and Valeriote proved in [3] that, for a finite idempotent algebra A, TCT type 1 occurs in the type set of V(A) iff there is a subalgebra B of A, and a congruence $\eta$, such that B$/\eta$ is a two element algebra with only the trivial projection operations.

No tractability without Taylor terms

If the algebra B, consisting of the set $\{0, 1\}$ along with trivial projection operations, occurs in the variety V(A), then the associated $\operatorname{CSP}(\langle A, \operatorname{Rel}(\mathbf{A})\rangle)$ is NP-complete.

(This is because the relational structure $\mathbf{S} = \langle \{0, 1\}, T\rangle$, with $T = \{0, 1\}^3 - \{(0,0,0), (1,1,1)\}$, that is has NP-complete $\operatorname{CSP}(\mathbf{S})$ and $$\operatorname{CSP}(\mathbf{S})\leq \operatorname{CSP}(\langle A, \operatorname{Rel}(\mathbf{A})\rangle).$$ It follows from the paragraph above that tractability of $\operatorname{CSP}(\langle A, \operatorname{Rel}(\mathbf{A})\rangle)$ necessitates a Taylor term operation in V(A).)
