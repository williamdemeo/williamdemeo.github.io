On a Problem of Palfy and Saxl

by William DeMeo - Feb 13th, 2014 -

The GitHub repsitory called PalfyAndSaxl contains notes on an approach I learned from Werner’s paper about graphical compositions. I think it may enable us to answer this nice question of theirs, which is: Given an $M_n$ lattice with at least $n\geq 3$ atoms. If 3 of the atoms pairwise permute, must they all pairwise permute?

Problem. Let A be a finite algebra with Con(A) isomorphic to M_n, for some n at least 4. If three nontrivial congruences of A pairwise permute, does it follow that every pair of congruences of A permute?

The main files in the repository are:

  1. PalfySaxlProblem.pdf – Notes on the problem.

  2. PalfySaxl-ConLatAndFactGroups.pdf – A scanned copy of the article by Palfy and Saxl, “Congruence lattices of finite algebras and factorizations of groups.” Comm. Algebra, 1990.

  3. Werner-PartLatConLat.pdf – A scanned copy of the article by Werner, “Which partition lattices are congruence lattices?”, Colloquia Mathematica, 1974.
